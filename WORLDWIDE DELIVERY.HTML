Inspired Mediterranean Fare from NYC's Flatiron District Since it first opened its doors in New York's Flatiron neighborhood in 2007, the elegant and upscale ilili has introduced countless New Yorkers to authentic Lebanese cuisine, perfected by the Massoud family in Beirut over the course of more than 100 years and today prepared with care by Owner and Executive Chef Philippe Massoud.

At ilili, Massoud expertly crafts a sophisticated blend of modern and traditional Lebanese cuisine, drawing influences from the span of the ancient Silk Road trade routes. From falafel and shawarma to zaatar-topped manousheh and even tender lamb shank braised with Levantine spices, this is a true taste of Lebanon, and it's shipping from New York straight to
